Altarflow replaces the shoebox of receipts and the end-of-month scramble with a single expense workflow your whole team can use. Snap a receipt, forward an invoice to your org's unique inbox, or connect your bank — AI agents handle the categorization and create the records. You review, approve, and move on. Every dollar lands in the right fund and flows directly into your financial reports with no extra steps.

## AI categorization

No matter how an expense enters Altarflow, an AI agent categorizes it before it ever reaches your review queue. The agent looks at the vendor name, transaction description, and any text on the receipt to assign the most likely category — Utilities, Maintenance, Supplies, Missions, and so on.

The categorization engine learns from every correction you make. Adjust a category once and the agent stores your preference. The next time a similar transaction comes in from the same vendor or with a similar description, it is already categorized correctly. The more you use Altarflow, the less you manually touch.

## Review and approval workflow

Altarflow separates expenses into two queues so you spend your time on what actually needs attention.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Agent suggestions queue">
    Expenses the AI categorized with high confidence appear here with a one-click **Approve** button. Review the suggested vendor, amount, category, and fund. If everything looks right, approve in a single click. You can approve an entire batch at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manual review tab">
    Expenses where OCR confidence was low, the bank description was ambiguous, or no prior preference exists are routed to the **Manual Review** tab. Open each record, confirm or correct the details, assign the right fund, and approve. Corrections teach the AI immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approved and filed">
    Once approved, the expense is locked to its category and fund. It appears in your expense reports, reduces the relevant fund balance, and is available for export — instantly, with no additional bookkeeping.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Fund allocation

Every expense in Altarflow is tagged to a fund — General Fund, Building Fund, Missions, Youth Ministry, or any custom fund your church uses. When you approve an expense, Altarflow updates the real-time balance for that fund immediately. There is no waiting for a monthly close.

Fund allocation turns expense tracking into true fund accounting. When a board member asks how much is left in the Building Fund, you can answer from a live dashboard instead of a spreadsheet. See the [Fund Allocation guide](/expenses/fund-allocation) for a full walkthrough.

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="Do I have to use all three capture methods?">
  No. Use whichever methods fit your workflow. Many churches start with OCR receipt scanning, then connect their bank account once they see how the categorization works. The email inbox is available to all plans and requires no setup beyond knowing your org's unique address.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Which plans include bank and credit card connection?">
  Bank and credit card sync is available on the **Growth** and **Scale** plans. OCR receipt scanning and email forwarding are available on all plans, including Seed. See [Plans and Pricing](/plans-and-pricing) for the full feature comparison.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if the AI categorizes something incorrectly?">
  Open the expense record, correct the category and fund, and save. The AI agent stores your correction as a preference and applies it to similar transactions going forward. You will see accuracy improve noticeably within the first few weeks of use.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does one-click approval work?">
  When an AI agent categorizes an expense with high confidence, it surfaces the suggested category, fund, vendor, and amount in the approval queue. If the suggestion is correct, click **Approve**. If you need to change anything, click into the record, edit, and then approve. You can also select multiple expenses and approve them all at once.
</Accordion>
