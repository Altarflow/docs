Altarflow's Web Portal is your church's home on the internet — a fully branded, customizable page where your community gives, signs up for events, submits forms, and engages with everything your church offers. You do not need a website, a developer, or a hosting plan. The portal is built into Altarflow and ready to customize the same afternoon you set it up.

<Note>
  The Web Portal is available on all Altarflow plans, including the Seed plan at $49/month. Every church gets a fully functional portal regardless of plan.
</Note>

## What the web portal is

Think of the web portal as the single link your church hands out — in the bulletin, on a card, via QR code, or programmed into an NFC tag on the pew. Anyone who taps or scans lands on your church's page, where they can donate, RSVP to an upcoming event, fill out a form, find your social media, or get directions.

The portal is not a multi-page website. It is an optimized single page designed to maximize action: every button is prominent, every link goes exactly where it needs to, and the experience works on any phone without an app download.

## What links you can add

Your portal can include any combination of the following:

- **Donation link** — connects directly to your Altarflow giving flow; donors can give with Apple Pay, Google Pay, card, or ACH
- **Public forms** — link to any form you have built in Altarflow (visitor card, prayer request, volunteer signup, baptism application)
- **Social media** — link to your church's Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, WhatsApp group, or any social account
- **Events** — add upcoming events with date, time, location, and a description; each event includes a **Google Maps link** for directions and a **tap-to-add-to-calendar** button so visitors can save it to their phone calendar in one tap

You can organize links into sections (for example: "Connect," "Give," "Upcoming Events") and choose a layout for each section independently.

## NFC tap programming

Altarflow NFC pods are physical tags you place anywhere in your building. When someone taps their phone to a pod, their browser opens to a specific section of your portal — no app required.

You program each NFC pod from your Altarflow dashboard:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a pod from your dashboard">
    Your dashboard lists every NFC pod associated with your church, identified by location name (e.g., "Welcome Desk," "Pew Row 5," "Lobby Stand").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a destination">
    Select which portal section or link the pod should open. A pod at the welcome desk might open the visitor form. A pod on the pew might open the giving flow. A pod at the door might open the event RSVP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and deploy">
    Changes take effect immediately. You do not need to reprogram the physical tag — the destination is stored in Altarflow, not on the tag itself.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassign anytime">
    If your Sunday schedule changes, a campaign ends, or you want to redirect traffic, change the pod's destination from your dashboard in under a minute. No tools, no IT staff, no new tags needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

NFC pods work on any modern iPhone or Android phone without any app download. Members simply hold their phone near the tag and the portal opens in their browser.

## Engagement analytics

Altarflow tracks every interaction with your portal so you can see what your community cares about and where attention drops off.

**Metrics tracked:**

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Page views** | Total number of times the portal was loaded |
| **Unique visitors** | Number of distinct people who visited |
| **Button clicks** | Which links and buttons your community taps most |
| **Bounce rate** | What percentage of visitors left without taking any action |
| **NFC pod performance** | Which physical pods are being used and how often |

Use analytics to optimize your portal over time. If the volunteer signup button gets few clicks despite being prominently placed, you may need to retitle it or move it up the page. If a particular NFC pod generates high engagement, consider adding similar pods nearby.

## QR codes and OG card generation

For every portal you publish, Altarflow generates:

- **A QR code** — download and use in printed bulletins, welcome cards, event flyers, and screens. The QR code always points to your live portal, so you do not need to reprint materials when you update the portal.
- **An OG (Open Graph) card** — a preview image that appears when someone shares your portal link in a text message, email, or social media post. The OG card shows your church name and hero image, making shared links look intentional and professional.

Both are available to download from your portal settings at any time.
