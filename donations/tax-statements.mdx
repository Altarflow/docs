Every donor who gives to your church needs a giving statement at tax time. Altarflow generates them automatically — accurate, formatted, and ready to distribute — without spreadsheets, mail merge templates, or manual addition. Because every donation from every source is tracked in one place, every statement Altarflow produces reflects the donor's complete giving history: digital, cash, Zelle, checks, and envelopes, all in one document. What used to take days before your January deadline now takes minutes.

## What's Included in a Giving Statement

Each giving statement Altarflow generates includes:

- **Donor name and address** as recorded in your CRM
- **Church name and EIN** for IRS documentation purposes
- **Every individual gift** for the calendar year, listed by date
- **Payment method** for each gift (digital, cash, check, Zelle, etc.)
- **Fund assignment** for each gift (General, Building, Missions, etc.)
- **Total given for the year** — calculated automatically, no manual math
- **A standard disclaimer** noting that no goods or services were received in exchange for the donations (required for charitable contribution deductions)

<Note>
Altarflow does not include individual gifts under $250 in the formal acknowledgment language, consistent with IRS requirements for written acknowledgment. The full itemized list still appears for the donor's reference.
</Note>

## Generating Statements

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Tax Statements">
    From your Altarflow dashboard, go to **Donations → Tax Statements**. Select the tax year you want to generate statements for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your scope">
    You can generate a statement for a **single donor** — useful when a member calls and needs theirs immediately — or generate statements for your **entire congregation** in one batch. Both options are available from the same screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and generate">
    Altarflow compiles the giving records and produces formatted PDF statements. Review a sample before generating the full batch to confirm the layout and totals look correct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Distribute to your congregation">
    Download individual PDFs for any donor, or export the full batch. Statements are also made available to donors directly through the donor portal — so many of your members will retrieve their own without ever contacting your office.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Formats

Altarflow generates giving statements as **formatted PDFs** suitable for printing, emailing as an attachment, or saving to a shared folder. Each PDF is named with the donor's name and the tax year for easy organization.

<Accordion title="Can I customize the statement layout?">
  You can add your church's name, address, and EIN to your Altarflow organization profile, and that information will appear on every statement automatically. Custom branding options — such as your church logo — are available from the organization settings page.
</Accordion>

## How Donors Access Their Own Statements

Donors do not need to wait for you to send them anything. Every donor who has made at least one digital gift through Altarflow can log in to **donor.altarflow.com** and download their own giving statements at any time — for the current year or any prior year on record.

<Tip>
Before your January distribution, send your congregation a quick reminder that they can retrieve their own giving statements anytime at **donor.altarflow.com**. Most members will pull their own documents without you needing to email or mail a single PDF — saving your office significant time and making donors feel empowered rather than dependent on the church office.
</Tip>

The donor portal is always available, year-round. A donor who needs their statement for a home loan application in April doesn't need to call your office — they log in and download it themselves.

## Best Practices for January Distribution

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Send by January 31" icon="calendar">
    The IRS requires written acknowledgment of donations of $250 or more to be provided before the donor files their tax return, or by January 31 — whichever is earlier. Aim to have statements available to your congregation in the first two weeks of January.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Announce from the pulpit" icon="microphone">
    On the first or second Sunday of January, let your congregation know statements are available in the donor portal. A 30-second announcement eliminates dozens of individual requests to the church office.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Email your donor list" icon="envelope">
    Send a brief email to all donors with the donor portal link. For donors who gave exclusively by cash or check and don't have a portal account, offer to email or print their statement directly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep prior years accessible" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    Altarflow retains giving records for all prior years. If a donor needs a statement from 2022 or 2023, you can generate it immediately — no digging through old files.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
Donors who gave exclusively through offline methods (cash, check, envelope) and have never made a digital gift will not have a donor portal account. For these donors, download and email or print their PDF statement directly from the Tax Statements page in your dashboard.
</Warning>
