When your church has a specific need — a new sound system, a building renovation, a mission trip — a focused campaign turns scattered individual gifts into a visible, momentum-building drive toward a shared goal. Altarflow lets you create a campaign in minutes, share it with your congregation, and watch progress update in real time as every digital donation, Zelle transfer, and cash gift comes in. One church used Altarflow campaigns to fund an entire LED screen renovation. You can do the same for whatever your community is rallying around.

## Creating a Campaign

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name your campaign">
    Give the campaign a clear, descriptive name your congregation will recognize — for example, "LED Screen Renovation," "Guatemala Mission Trip 2025," or "New Roof Fund." The name appears on the giving page donors see when they contribute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a goal amount and deadline">
    Enter the total amount you need to raise and the date by which you'd like to reach it. Altarflow displays progress as a percentage toward the goal, which motivates donors to give when they can see how close the campaign is to the finish line.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a fund">
    Link the campaign to the fund where these donations belong — General, Building, Missions, or a custom fund you've created. Every gift made through this campaign is automatically tagged to that fund in your records.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and get the campaign link">
    Once you save the campaign, Altarflow generates a unique giving link for it. Share this link anywhere — in your Sunday bulletin, a text message, your church's website, or a social media post.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sharing the Campaign

Your campaign link opens a focused giving page that shows the campaign name, goal, current progress, and a Quick Give form. Donors can give via Apple Pay, Google Pay, or a stored card without leaving the page.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sunday Bulletin" icon="newspaper">
    Print the campaign URL or QR code in your weekly bulletin so in-person attendees can give on the spot during the service.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Text & Email" icon="envelope">
    Send the link directly to your congregation via group text or email. Donors tap the link, see the progress, and give in seconds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Church Website" icon="globe">
    Embed the campaign link on your church's website or web portal so anyone who visits can contribute to the active campaign.
  </Card>
  <Card title="NFC Tap Tags" icon="wifi">
    Point one of your NFC tap tags to the campaign link from your dashboard. Place the tag at the entrance or offering station during the campaign period.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Real-Time Progress Tracking

Every donation made through the campaign link updates the campaign's total instantly. From your Altarflow dashboard, you can see:

- **Total raised** toward the goal at any moment
- **Number of donors** who have contributed
- **Progress percentage** — a clear visual of how close you are
- **Individual gift details** — each donation listed with donor name, amount, method, and date

You can share the progress view with your leadership team or display it during services to build momentum as the campaign approaches its goal.

## Attributing Digital Donations to a Campaign

When a donor gives through your campaign link, that gift is automatically attributed to the campaign and tagged to the associated fund. It also appears in your main donations table like any other gift — with the campaign name noted on the record — so it's counted in both your campaign total and your overall giving reports.

## Adding Offline Gifts to a Campaign

Not every contribution will come through the digital link. When a donor hands you a check or cash specifically for the campaign, you add it manually:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the manual donation form">
    Go to your Donations table and select **Add Donation**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the gift details">
    Enter the donor, amount, payment method (cash or check), and date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the campaign">
    Select the campaign from the campaign dropdown. The gift is added to the campaign total and attributed to the correct fund immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example Use Cases

<Accordion title="Building renovation — LED screen">
  One Altarflow church launched a campaign to fund a new LED screen in their sanctuary. They shared the campaign link in the Sunday bulletin and by group text. Congregation members gave digitally throughout the week between services, and the pastor announced the live progress total each Sunday. The campaign reached its goal before the deadline — fully funded, every gift tracked, and the fund ready to pay the contractor.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Mission trip fundraising">
  A church sending a team to Guatemala created a campaign with a deadline two weeks before the trip's departure. Team members shared the link personally with friends and family. Cash and check donations from in-person supporters were added manually. Every gift — digital and offline — counted toward the total, and the team left fully funded.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Emergency needs and special offerings">
  When a family in the congregation faces a sudden hardship, a campaign lets the church rally quickly. Create a campaign, share the link during announcements, and watch the giving roll in. The church can see in real time when enough has been raised and close the campaign when the need is met.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
Keep your campaign names specific and your deadlines visible. Campaigns with a clear goal and a real deadline consistently raise more than open-ended giving appeals — people respond when they can see the finish line.
</Tip>
