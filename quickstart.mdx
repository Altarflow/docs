Getting Altarflow up and running is designed to take an afternoon, not a week. The steps below walk you through everything from creating your account to having AI agents actively reviewing transactions. Most churches complete the full setup in under two hours — including importing years of historical data.

<Note>
  Setup typically takes **under 2 hours** from account creation to your first live transaction. Free data migration of up to 5 years of donor and giving history is included on every plan.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Book a demo and create your account">
    Start at [altarflow.com/contact](https://altarflow.com/contact) to book a 15-minute demo. The demo is a working session — bring your current spreadsheet, your toughest reconciliation problem, or just a list of questions. The Altarflow team will map what you're doing today, show you exactly where the platform takes the work off your plate, and set up your account on the call.

    <Tip>
      You don't need to prepare anything special. A rough picture of how your church currently handles donations and expenses is enough to make the demo useful.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Migrate your existing data">
    Once your account is active, Altarflow migrates your existing donor records, giving history, and expense data for free — up to five years back. You don't need to export CSVs or reformat anything yourself. The migration team handles the import whether your data lives in ChurchTrac, Breeze, Tithely, Planning Center, QuickBooks, or a spreadsheet.

    After migration, your donor profiles, fund totals, and historical reports are available immediately. Nothing is left behind.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your bank accounts and credit cards">
    Navigate to **Settings → Bank Connections** and link every account your church uses. Altarflow connects via a secure read-only bank integration that retrieves your transaction history automatically.

    Once connected, AI agents begin processing your transaction feed right away — categorizing expenses, flagging Zelle and Venmo transfers, and building the expense queue for your review. Connect as many accounts and credit cards as you need; there is no per-account fee.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Activate Quick Give digital giving">
    Go to **Giving → Quick Give** to generate your church's giving page. Your page is live at `app.altarflow.com/your-church` the moment you activate it. Donors can give via Apple Pay, Google Pay, stored card, or ACH — in under 5 seconds.

    From this screen you can also:
    - Print or download your giving page QR code for bulletins and screens
    - Order NFC tap tags for pews, welcome desks, and kiosks
    - Configure giving funds so donors can designate their gift

    Digital donations processed through Altarflow are subject to a **4% processing fee**. You receive daily payouts via Stripe — funds hit your account the next business day.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite your team members">
    Go to **Settings → Team** and invite your pastor, treasurer, finance volunteer, or any administrator who needs access. Altarflow supports unlimited admins on every plan — there is no per-seat charge.

    Each team member receives a login invitation by email and can be assigned access appropriate to their role. You stay in control of who can approve transactions, view reports, or manage settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and approve AI agent work">
    Once your bank is connected, the AI agents get to work immediately. Navigate to **Agents → Review Queue** to see everything they've done:

    - Expenses pulled from your bank feed, each with a suggested category and fund
    - Zelle and Venmo transfers matched to donor profiles
    - Receipts scanned and filed from forwarded emails or photo uploads

    Review the queue, correct anything the agents got wrong, and click **Approve All** to confirm their work in one step. Every correction you make teaches the agents — the same transaction type will be handled correctly next time without your input.

    <Tip>
      The first review session is the longest. After two to four weeks of corrections, most churches find the queue accurate enough that approval takes under 20 minutes a month.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
