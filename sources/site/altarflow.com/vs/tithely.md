Tithely has grown from a giving tool into a suite — giving, ChMS (the acquired Breeze), a church app, a website, worship tools. But at $119/mo All-Access, it still doesn't include fund accounting, AI categorization, or OCR receipt scanning. Altarflow does — for less.

## What is Altarflow?

Altarflow is the financial operating system for churches. Donations, expenses, reporting, and digital giving in one platform — not five. AI agents categorize transactions, match Zelle and Venmo gifts to donors, and scan receipts in English and Spanish. From $99/month, replacing the giving-platform-plus-QuickBooks-plus-receipt-tool stack most churches patch together.

The short answer

Tithely is a giving platform that grew into a bundle. Altarflow is a financial operating system that happens to include a CRM.

Who should pick what

Pick Tithely for a custom branded app, free-tier giving, and a broad product bundle. Pick Altarflow when your treasurer needs AI help on expenses, fund accounting, and donor matching.

Bottom line

Tithely All-Access runs $119/mo and still needs QuickBooks for fund accounting. Altarflow is $99/mo with AI categorization, OCR receipts, and fund accounting built in — plus daily Stripe payouts instead of Tithely's 7–14 day ACH wait.
