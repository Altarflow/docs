A donor profile in Altarflow is the complete financial record of a person's relationship with your church. It captures every gift regardless of how it was given — Apple Pay, Zelle, check, cash envelope, or any other method — and presents the full picture in one place. Profiles build themselves as giving flows through the platform, so your records are always current without manual data entry.

## How donor profiles are created

Altarflow creates and updates donor profiles through three automatic pathways:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Digital giving">
    When a donor gives through Quick Give — using Apple Pay, Google Pay, a stored card, or ACH — Altarflow creates their profile on their first transaction using their name, phone number, and email. Every subsequent gift from any Altarflow-connected giving page is added to that profile automatically, even if they give to a different church in the Altarflow network.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zelle and Venmo matching">
    When the AI matching agent pairs a Zelle or Venmo transfer with a donor, the donation is added to that donor's profile. If no match exists, the agent creates a new profile stub with the available information (name from the transfer, amount, date) and flags it for your review. Once you confirm the match, all future transfers from that source are attributed automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Form submissions">
    When a member fills out a form — a new-visitor card, an event RSVP, a volunteer signup — their submission creates or updates their CRM profile. That profile is the same record that stores their giving history, so contact information, ministry involvement, and financial history are all on one page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also create profiles manually for donors who give only by cash or check. Navigate to **Donors → New Donor**, enter their information, and start logging their gifts against that profile immediately.

## What's on a donor profile

Every donor profile surfaces the following information in one view:

| Section | What you see |
|---|---|
| **Contact information** | Name, phone, email, address, preferred language |
| **Giving summary** | Total given to date, total this year, average gift size, giving frequency |
| **Giving history** | Every donation, sorted by date, with source (digital, Zelle, cash, check), amount, and fund |
| **Fund breakdown** | Total given to each fund — General, Building, Missions, etc. |
| **Campaign participation** | Every campaign the donor has contributed to and their total per campaign |
| **Tax statements** | Generated year-end giving statements, available for download at any time |
| **CRM fields** | Family grouping, custom fields, notes, ministry involvement |

<Tip>
  The fund breakdown view is especially useful before a capital campaign or a year-end giving push. You can quickly see which donors have historically given to a specific fund and prioritize your outreach accordingly.
</Tip>

## How donors access their own data

Every donor who has given digitally through Altarflow has access to a self-service portal at **[donor.altarflow.com](https://donor.altarflow.com)**. They log in with their phone number — no password required — and see:

- Every donation to every Altarflow-connected organization they've supported
- A complete giving history with dates, amounts, and fund designations
- Downloadable year-end tax statements for any year
- Stored payment methods (up to three cards)
- The ability to give again with a single tap

The donor portal removes the most common administrative request pastors receive — "Can you send me my giving statement?" — by putting that document in the donor's hands year-round. Your staff doesn't need to generate or email statements on request.

<Note>
  The donor portal is available on the Growth and Scale plans. Donors on Seed-plan churches receive manually generated tax statements instead.
</Note>

## Donor profiles and the member CRM

Donor profiles are not separate from your CRM — they are the same record. Every person in Altarflow has one profile that stores both their giving history and their ministry engagement. This means:

- A family's giving rolls up to a single household record with individual gift attribution
- Pastoral notes, custom fields, and ministry team assignments sit next to financial history
- Full-text search across names, phone numbers, and giving history finds anyone in under a second
- Reports that combine giving patterns with ministry involvement are possible without exporting anything

When a new visitor fills out a form on Sunday, their profile is created. When they give for the first time the following Sunday, that donation is added to the same profile — automatically, because Altarflow matched their phone number across both events.

## Donor privacy and data ownership

Your donor data belongs to your church. Altarflow does not sell, share, or use your donor information for any purpose outside of operating the platform. Donors who use the self-service portal can see their giving history and payment methods — but they cannot see other donors' records or your church's internal financial data.

<Accordion title="Can a donor request their data be deleted?">
  Yes. You can delete or anonymize any donor profile from the profile's settings menu. Donations associated with the profile are retained in aggregate for your financial records, but the personal identifiers are removed. Contact Altarflow support if you need assistance with a bulk deletion request.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens to donor profiles if we cancel Altarflow?">
  You can export your full donor and giving history at any time from **Settings → Export**. All data is available in CSV format. Altarflow retains your data for 90 days after cancellation, after which it is permanently deleted.
</Accordion>
