Once Altarflow generates a report, you can get it to any audience in the format they actually need. Board members get a clean, print-ready PDF. Your accountant or bookkeeper gets a CSV they can open directly in QuickBooks or Excel. Leadership can follow along with a live link that always shows the latest numbers. And for bilingual churches, every report flips between English and Spanish with a single toggle — no duplicate work required.

<Note>
  Bilingual report output — the ability to toggle between English and Spanish before exporting or sharing — is available on all Altarflow plans, including Seed. You do not need to upgrade to use bilingual reports.
</Note>

## PDF export for board meetings

The PDF export produces a formatted, print-ready document that looks like it was designed for a board packet — because it was. It includes your church name, the report type, the selected date range, and a clean layout with totals, charts, and line-item detail.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate your report">
    Open **Reports**, choose a report type, set your date range and any filters, then click **Generate Report**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select language">
    Use the bilingual toggle to choose **English** or **Spanish** before exporting. This sets the language for all labels, headings, and field names in the PDF.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export → PDF">
    Select **Export** from the toolbar, then choose **PDF**. The file downloads to your device immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach to your board packet">
    The PDF is self-contained and ready to attach to an email, upload to a shared drive, or print for the meeting. No reformatting needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## CSV export for accountants

The CSV export contains raw row-level data — every transaction, donor record, or expense line item that makes up the report. This is the format your accountant, bookkeeper, or auditor will want when they need to work with the numbers directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate and filter your report">
    Apply any date, fund, or category filters before exporting so the CSV contains exactly the data your accountant needs — not the entire dataset.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export → CSV">
    Select **Export**, then choose **CSV**. The file downloads immediately and can be opened in Excel, Google Sheets, or imported into QuickBooks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sharing a live link

Instead of exporting a file, you can share a URL that always reflects the current data. Anyone with the link sees the report as it stands at the moment they open it — including any transactions recorded after you sent the link.

This is useful for:

- Letting your pastor or board chair check numbers between meetings without asking you to regenerate the report
- Giving your treasurer ongoing visibility without granting them full dashboard access
- Running a capital campaign where leadership wants to watch giving progress in real time

To generate a live link, click **Share** from the report toolbar and copy the URL. You can regenerate or revoke links at any time from your dashboard.

## Bilingual toggle: English and Spanish

Every Altarflow report — donation, expense, and financial analysis — supports bilingual output. Before you export or share, use the **EN / ES toggle** to select the language. The toggle changes all field labels, column headers, section titles, and navigation text. The underlying data (donor names, dollar amounts, dates) is unchanged.

Common use cases:

| Audience | Recommended approach |
|---|---|
| English-only board | Export PDF in English |
| Spanish-speaking finance committee | Export PDF in Spanish |
| Bilingual leadership team | Share live link — each member reads in their language |
| External accountant | Export CSV (language-neutral data) |

## Best practices by audience

**For your board or elder council** — Export a PDF snapshot the day before the meeting and include it in the meeting packet. This ensures everyone is reviewing identical numbers in the official record. If a board member wants to dig deeper between meetings, share a live link as a follow-up.

**For your accountant or bookkeeper** — Use the CSV export filtered to the exact period and categories they need. If your accountant works quarterly, set up a recurring reminder to export at the end of each quarter.

**For your pastor or senior leadership** — Share a live link to the Financial Analysis report. They can check it anytime without asking you for an update, and the numbers are always current.

**For a grant application or external funder** — Export a PDF in English (or Spanish if appropriate for the funder). The formatted layout looks professional and requires no additional design work on your part.

**For church members** — If your church practices financial transparency, the live link can be shared with the congregation. Members can see giving totals and how funds are used without accessing any admin functions.
